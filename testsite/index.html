About Our Lab

The Labour Process and Technology Lab (LPT Lab) explores the intersection of work, automation, and digital transformation. We study how new technologies shape workers' lives, redefine power dynamics in the workplace, and influence collective action. Through a combination of critical research and participatory inquiry, we examine how unions, worker movements, and other collective forms of resistance engage with, adapt to, and challenge technological shifts in the workplace. Our goal is to imagine and create more equitable, just, and worker-centered futures in the digital age.

The LPT lab is part of the Faculty of Information at the University of Toronto.

Our Work

“Through our labor, we create the world and we create ourselves.” — Harry Braverman

Our key areas of inquiry include:

  • The impact of automation on workplace practices and worker agency
  • Digital platforms and the reshaping of labour markets
  • Data-driven management and algorithmic decision-making
  • Historical and comparative analyses of labour process theory

Meet Our Team

Our interdisciplinary team includes researchers, students, and practitioners specializing in information science, sociology, and critical technology studies. Together, we aim to produce impactful scholarship and inform public debates on the changing nature of work.
